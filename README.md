```markdown name=types-of-cyberattacks.md
# Types of Cyberattacks

Cyberattacks are malicious attempts to access, damage, disrupt, or steal data from computer systems, networks, or devices. Understanding different types of cyberattacks is crucial for implementing effective security measures. Here are some common types:

## 1. Malware Attacks
Malware refers to malicious software designed to harm or exploit any programmable device, service, or network. Common types include viruses, worms, trojans, ransomware, spyware, and adware.

## 2. Phishing Attacks
Phishing involves tricking people into revealing sensitive information (like passwords or credit card numbers) by pretending to be a trustworthy entity, often via email or fake websites.

## 3. Denial-of-Service (DoS) & Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) Attacks
DoS attacks flood a network or website with traffic, making it unavailable to users. DDoS attacks are similar but use multiple compromised systems to amplify the impact.

## 4. Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) Attacks
In MitM attacks, the attacker secretly intercepts and relays messages between two parties who believe they are communicating directly with each other.

## 5. SQL Injection Attacks
SQL injection targets databases by inserting malicious SQL queries into input fields, allowing attackers to access or manipulate data.

## 6. Cross-Site Scripting (XSS)
XSS attacks inject malicious scripts into websites, which execute in the browsers of users who visit the affected pages, often stealing information or performing actions on their behalf.

## 7. Password Attacks
Attackers try to obtain passwords through brute-force, dictionary attacks, or credential stuffing to gain unauthorized access.

## 8. Zero-Day Exploits
These attacks target vulnerabilities in software that are unknown to the vendor. Since the flaw is not yet patched, attackers exploit it before a fix is available.

## 9. Insider Threats
Insider attacks are carried out by someone within the organization who has access to sensitive data or systems, often motivated by personal gain or revenge.

## 10. Drive-By Download Attacks
Users unintentionally download malicious software by visiting compromised websites or clicking on infected ads or links.

## 11. Social Engineering Attacks
Social engineering manipulates people into divulging confidential information, often by impersonating authority figures or exploiting human psychology.

## 12. Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs)
APTs are prolonged and targeted cyberattacks where an intruder gains access to a network and remains undetected for an extended period, stealing data or causing damage.

---

**Protecting against cyberattacks requires a combination of technology, awareness, and best practices. Regular updates, strong passwords, security training, and monitoring can help mitigate risks.**
```
